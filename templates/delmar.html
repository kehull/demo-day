Case Study:

Delmar Loop
St. Louis, MO

Delmar Loop was hit hard by COVID-19.

Long before COVID-19 even existed, the street was plagued with restaurants and businesses shutting

A new mini golf course project was abandoned. Restaurants such as Blue Box Pizza, The Melting Pot, Hopcat, Tavolo V, and Guerilla Street Food all closed in 2020. Peacock Diner drastically reduced their hours. Blueberry Hill shut down entirely for several months, as did Moonrise Hotel and Pin-Up Bowl.

Delmar Loop is also home to a number of small coffee shops and cafes that seemed like they might not survive pandemic shut-downs, especially with the college dorms surrounding the area sitting empty. But, over one year later, ALL of the little cafes are still thriving:

This case study is pure anecdote, of course, but it proves that even in times of hardship, people who can afford coffee and desserts will seek out those small comforts to get through the day. Furthermore, it demonstrates an important point about choosing a location for a cafe: local competition is a good thing!

If there's a Starbucks nearby, you can bet that there's a big market for a cafe in that area- bigger than the Starbucks alone can handle. Don't believe me? Think about the last time you tried to order at a Starbucks drive-thru at rush hour. Now think about all your friends who drink coffee, tea, smoothies, and enjoy baked goods, but who stick their nose up at the idea of setting foot in a chain coffee shop, even though Starbucks is the nearest cafe? Your market is right under Starbuck's nose.
