Case Study: Show Me Costa Rica Coffee

Investigating potential locations for this company to open their first storefront

Case Study

Show Me
Costa Rica Coffee

Scouting locations for an established coffee brand looking to open their first brick-and-mortar store.

Who is Show Me Costa Rica Coffee?

The Show Me Costa Rica Project is a non-profit organization founded in St. Louis, MO. They focus on selling premium coffee from Costa Rican farms, as well as selling Pura Vida merchandise in order to bring equity in for underprivileged communities. The funds are used to give high school students the opportunity to travel internationally to continue their learning. Currently, the Show Me Costa Rica Project has been selling their products in the Tower Grove area and hoping to open a permanent location in a desirable area.

So where does Costa Rica fit in St. Louis?

After reviewing our map, it might seem like the best areas for a new coffee location would be the ones circled in this map- These include areas such as Jennings (which has no current Starbucks or major coffee chain location) or the St. Charles area (which is lacking in Starbucks stores), but overwhelmed with McDonaldâ€™s. However, a lack of "third-place" type chain shops actually tends to indicate a risky market, or even a dud market, rather than an area of untapped potential for a new location.

However, we posit that one of the best potential locations for a new coffee house would be the area near the intersection of Historic 66 and Watson. This area is in close proximity to Tower Grove, where Show Me Costa Rica Coffee already have an established following. Surrounding businesses such as Starbucks, Panera Bread, and IHOP show a strong cafe demographic, and nearby local businesses like Blueprint Coffee, Ted Drewes, and Donut Drive-In indicate a thriving scene of supportive customers. While the chain stores provide "third-place" type environments and the local shops offer high-quality to-go options, there doesn't appear to be a local shop that provides both a high-quality menu AND a homey cafe atmosphere where a person could spend a few hours working on their laptop or chatting with a friend. While there certainly are other areas in St. Louis where a new coffee shop could thrive, we would start by looking for locations here.
